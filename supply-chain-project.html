Global Supply Chain Performance & Cost Optimization

An interactive dashboard to monitor global logistics, identify underperforming shipments, and support cost negotiations.

Objective

To design and implement an analytical tool for a global logistics team to provide insights into delivery performance and cost efficiency.

Tools Used

Tableau Python SQL

Data Source

SCMS_Delivery_History_Dataset

The Final Dashboard

The Process: From Raw Data to Insight

This project began with the challenge of consolidating complex logistics data from multiple sources into a single, coherent view. The goal was to empower the vendor management team with actionable insights.

1. Data Cleaning and Preparation

The initial dataset contained inconsistencies in vendor naming, varied date formats, and missing shipping cost values. I used Python (Pandas) to standardize vendor names, normalize date fields, and impute missing costs based on historical averages for similar routes and product types. This step was critical to ensure the accuracy of the performance metrics.

2. Feature Engineering

To provide deeper insights, I engineered several key metrics that were not present in the original data. These included:

  • On-Time Delivery (OTD) Rate: Calculated by comparing actual delivery dates to planned delivery dates.
  • Delivery Lead Time: The total time from shipment creation to final delivery.
  • Cost Per Shipment: A standardized metric to compare cost-efficiency across different vendors and routes.

3. Key Analytical Findings

Using the cleaned and enriched data, I performed an in-depth analysis that formed the backbone of the dashboard. A key finding was that two specific product groups, despite being a small portion of total shipments, were responsible for nearly 99% of the total logistics costs due to specialized handling requirements. This insight, highlighted in the dashboard, immediately shifted the focus of cost-saving initiatives.

Conclusion & Impact

The final deliverable was an analytical tool that provided the logistics team with unprecedented visibility into their supply chain. It is now used for ongoing vendor management, identifying underperforming shipments in near real-time, and provides concrete data to support cost negotiation with shipping partners.
